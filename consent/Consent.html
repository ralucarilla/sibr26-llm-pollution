4. What will happen to the information and data collected?

All data collected from you will be stored and scientifically analyzed in accordance with data protection laws (EU General Data Protection Regulation [GDPR], German Federal Data Protection Act). The study will be hosted on servers of the Max Planck Society.

No contact information (name, address, phone number, email address, etc.) will be collected in the study. If you choose to participate in the study, we will link broad information about your age, gender, ethnicity, political ideology, income, and education level to your answers throughout the survey. Your Prolific ID will be stored separately from the study data for two years and will be used for payment purposes. The study data will be stored under a unique code number so that it cannot be linked to you during analysis. The code number is stored on a separate code list in a secure location and is only accessible to a limited number of people working on the project. The code list is the only link between your Prolific ID and your study data. After two years, the code list will be deleted, and it will no longer be possible to link the study data to you. The study data will be stored for at least 10 years in accordance with the Good Scientific Practice of the German Research Foundation and the rules of the Max Planck Society.

At the end of the data collection, it is planned to upload the research data (excluding your Prolific ID and any identifiers) to the Open Science Framework (https://osf.io/) for archiving and further scientific use. This will allow other researchers to verify or replicate the results of the study and improve the quality of scientific research. The study data can also be used for new research questions beyond the purpose of this study. The Open Science Framework platform is located outside the European Union. Once uploaded there, the data will be made publicly available worldwide, i.e., the data can be viewed, downloaded, and used by interested parties. This also means that the data leaves the scope of European data protection law and that you may not be able to enforce your rights as a data subject. From the time of publication on the platform, the use of this data can no longer be controlled, subsequently restricted or deleted anywhere, and this is beyond the control or responsibility of the MPIB.

The results of the study will only be published without direct reference to you. If the results of the study are published, your identity will remain confidential. This means that it will not be possible to tell from the results which person provided the information, nor will it be possible to tell from the research data that you took part in a study, unless you have given separate consent for personal publication. We will capture the contents in any text interaction with any of the shopping assistance tool for analysis. Your texts will be securely stored on the servers of the Max Planck Institute for Human Development (MPIB). Access to these texts is strictly limited to the research team involved in this study.

5. Legal basis

The legal basis for the processing of your data is your consent, in accordance with Article 6(1)(a) of the GDPR. You may withdraw your consent at any time with effect for the future. You may also withdraw your consent to the processing and use of your data at any time without negative consequences. Please note that once the code list has been deleted (see Section 4), it is no longer possible to link your Prolific ID to your study data.

6. Data subject rights

You have the following rights with regard to your personal data:

  • Right of access, Art. 15 GDPR
  • Right of rectification, Art. 16 GDPR
  • Right to erasure, Art. 17 GDPR
  • Right to data portability, Art. 20 GDPR
  • Right to restriction of processing, Art. 18 GDPR
  • Right to object to data processing or use, Art. 21 GDPR.

Finally, you may complain to the competent data protection authority about what you consider to be unlawful data processing. The competent authority for the Max Planck Society is the Bavarian Data Protection Authority, P.O. Box 606, 91511 Ansbach, Germany.

7. Participation is voluntary

Participation in this study is voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time without giving any reason and without any negative consequences. You may also withdraw your consent to data processing and usage at any time with effect for the future and without negative consequences. To do this, please contact the researcher through Prolific direct message or email the responsible researcher, **RESEARCHER NAME**, at ****@mpib-berlin.mpg.de. Please note that since no personally identifiable information is collected, it will not be possible to link your study data to your person. Hence it will not be possible to withdraw your consent to further processing your study data as described above once the study is completed.
